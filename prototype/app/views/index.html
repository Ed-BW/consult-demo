Key findings

Smokefree generation:

63% of respondents are in favour of the smokefree generation policy, 33% are opposed

The key argument expressed in the responses is that smoking is a major health issue in the UK and that strict measures need to be implemented to reduce its impact.

Product scope:

63% of respondents agree that all tobacco products, cigarette papers and herbal smoking products should be covered in the new legislation, 31% are opposed

Respondents express support in order to eradicate smoking and protect public health. Respondents also commonly agree that all tobacco products should be covered to promote a complete ban on smoking and prevent loopholes that can be exploited by the tobacco industry.

Disposable vapes:

79% of respondents agree that there should be restrictions on the sale and supply of disposable vapes, 18% are opposed

Respondents cite negative impacts on the environment and on children as the key reasons for support.

Enforcement:

88% of respondents think that that fixed penalty notices should be issued for breaches of age of sale legislation for tobacco products and vapes, 9% are opposed

Respondents commonly believe that FPNs would help restrict the sale of these products to minors and make retailers and sellers comply with the law.
